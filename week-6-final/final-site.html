Online Usability Study

I have been responsible for planning an online usability study for the early version of an iOS application.

I identified research questions, as well as formulated tasks to get answers to those research questions. Tasks were created with the intention of not leading the user to a desired result:

1. Use the pitch.Me iPhone application to add information about you and your business. User Goals: Sign up, navigate to profile, and update profile.

2. Add a few things you are interested in to your profile. User Goals: User adds interests to their profile.

3. Imageine as a business owner, you desire some product or service, using the pitch.Me application, post about the product or service you desire. User Goals: User posts a need about the product or service.

Chatbot User Flow

Competitive Research

In order to prepare for designing a chatbot experience, I performed competitive research to establish a document of best practices for chatbot UX. Sources that proved useful were Facebook Messenger, Slack, and Microsoft Documentation. The document I created has been referenced to while our team has begun chatbot design work.

User Flow

My teammate and I put together end to end user flows for how interacting with a chatbot will work throughout our application. I provided best practices from competitive research, and enforced usability principles of interaction design to help make the easiest flow for users.
